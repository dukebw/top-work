Margherita Frittata

Description

If you love the flavors of Margherita Pizza or Caprese Salad (tomatoes, basil and fresh mozzarella cheese), then give this simple, summery frittata a try! Serve it with a colorful green salad for lunch, brunch or dinner.

Ingredients

  • 8 large eggs
  • 3 tbsp milk or cream
  • Salt and freshly ground black pepper (about ½ tsp each)
  • 2 tsp olive oil or butter
  • 3.5 oz/100 g soft, FRESH mozzarella cheese, sliced
  • 1 plum tomato, very thinly sliced
  • 2 tbsp basil pesto (see variation below for non-pesto version)
  • Chopped fresh basil for garnish (pictured)

Steps

  1. In a medium bowl, whisk together eggs, milk, salt and pepper until well blended and a bit frothy.
  2. Heat olive oil in non-stick skillet over medium heat.
  3. Add egg mixture.
  4. Reduce heat to medium-low, cover and cook for 5 to 7 minutes, until edges look like they’re beginning to set.
  5. Using a rubber spatula, raise the edges of the frittata so that any uncooked egg runs underneath.
  6. Cover and cook 2 to 3 more minutes, until top is still a bit wet but almost cooked through.
  7. Meanwhile, preheat oven broiler but leave rack in center position.
  8. Layer slices of mozzarella and tomatoes over eggs in skillet.
  9. Dot with small dollops of pesto.
  10. Transfer skillet to oven and broil frittata for 1 to 2 minutes, until cheese is melted and eggs are completely cooked and slightly browned around edges. Be careful not to burn it!
  11. Remove frittata from oven and sprinkle with fresh basil (optional).
  12. Let cool 5 minutes before slicing and serving. (This frittata tastes better and is easier to slice when it’s warm and not hot.)
